Project Description

Emerging markets are witnessing rapid economic growth and urbanization, leading to an increased demand for electricity. However, the existing power infrastructure in these regions often struggles to keep pace with the surging energy requirements. Insufficient power supply poses significant challenges, hindering industrial growth, affecting daily lives, and limiting opportunities for socioeconomic progress. The aim of this project is to address power shortage challenges in emerging markets by relocating two existing gas power stations (70-80% new) from China. This strategic initiative forms part of our energy investments agenda, providing a quick solution for immediate power needs while fostering sustainable development. By leveraging the expertise and infrastructure already established within us, this project aims to expedite the deployment of reliable and cleaner energy sources in the targeted emerging markets.

Automated Solar PV Assembly and Production

Green Infrastructure and Power Investments PLC is pioneering innovation in renewable energy with its cutting-edge Automated Solar PV Assembly and Production facility. This state-of-the-art production line represents a significant advancement in the manufacturing of solar photovoltaic (PV) panels, leveraging the latest in automation technologies to enhance both efficiency and scalability.

The facility incorporates a range of advanced automated processes, including robotic assembly lines, precision laser cutting, and automated quality control systems. These technologies work in concert to significantly reduce production costs and cycle times. By automating labor-intensive and repetitive tasks, the facility not only increases throughput but also ensures consistent product quality and minimizes human error.

One of the key advantages of this automated approach is the ability to rapidly scale production to meet the growing global demand for renewable energy. With the global push towards sustainable energy solutions, the need for high-quality solar panels has never been greater. The automated production line allows for quick adjustments to production volumes, ensuring that supply can keep pace with market demand.

Furthermore, the integration of advanced automation leads to substantial cost reductions. Lower production costs enable Green Infrastructure and Power Investments PLC to offer competitively priced solar panels, making solar energy more accessible and affordable. This affordability is crucial for accelerating the adoption of solar energy, especially in emerging markets where cost remains a significant barrier.

This initiative underscores Green Infrastructure and Power Investments PLC’s commitment to sustainable energy solutions and reinforces its leadership in the renewable energy sector. By investing in cutting-edge production technology, the company is not only enhancing its operational capabilities but also contributing to the broader goal of reducing global carbon emissions through increased solar energy deployment.

HVDC Submarine Transmission Line

Green Infrastructure and Power Investments PLC is at the forefront of modern energy transmission with the development of a High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) Submarine Transmission Line. This groundbreaking project is designed to facilitate the efficient and reliable transfer of large volumes of electricity across vast distances, including undersea routes.

HVDC technology is well-suited for long-distance electricity transmission due to its ability to minimize energy losses compared to traditional alternating current (AC) transmission systems. By using direct current, the HVDC lines can transmit power over longer distances with higher efficiency and reduced losses. This makes HVDC an ideal solution for connecting offshore renewable energy sources, such as wind farms and solar arrays, to onshore power grids.

The submarine aspect of the HVDC transmission line allows it to traverse underwater routes, which is particularly advantageous for connecting islands, offshore platforms, and other remote locations to the main power grid. This capability is essential for tapping into offshore renewable energy resources, which are often located far from where the energy is ultimately needed.

By implementing HVDC technology, Green Infrastructure and Power Investments PLC is addressing some of the key challenges in modern energy transmission, such as stability and efficiency. HVDC systems are known for their ability to provide stable and reliable power supply, even over long distances. This stability is crucial for maintaining a consistent energy flow and preventing outages, thereby enhancing the overall reliability of the power grid.

This strategic investment in HVDC technology reflects the company’s dedication to enhancing energy infrastructure and promoting sustainable power solutions. By facilitating the efficient transfer of renewable energy, Green Infrastructure and Power Investments PLC is playing a pivotal role in the transition to a sustainable energy future.

Quality Assurance

Quality assurance is crucial for a used gas turbine. Prior to commissioning, we will refurbish and ensure that the gas turbine has a minimum operational life of 25 years. Thus, it provides the following advantages:


  • Reliability: Refurbished gas turbines will have a higher level of reliability. By conducting repairs and replacing critical components, the machine's stability and performance during operation are ensured.

  • Extended lifespan: Through refurbishment, the operational lifespan of the gas turbine can be extended. Repairs and updates to key parts and systems can guarantee continued efficient operation of the machine in the future.

  • Performance optimization: The refurbishment process allows for performance optimization of the gas turbine. By utilizing the latest technologies and processes, the efficiency and performance of the turbine can be improved to meet evolving demands.

  • Compliance with standards: During the refurbishment process, it is ensured that the gas turbine complies with relevant industry standards and regulatory requirements. This includes safety standards, environmental requirements, as well as operating and maintenance guidelines.

Advantages of using a used gas turbine

  1. Cost savings: One of the primary advantages of purchasing a used gas turbine is the significant cost savings compared to buying a new one. Used turbines are generally more affordable and can provide a cost-effective solution for power generation or other industrial applications.

  2. Shorter lead time: Acquiring a new gas turbine can involve a lengthy procurement process, including manufacturing, testing, and delivery. By opting for a used turbine, you can significantly reduce the lead time and quickly get the equipment up and running.

  3. Reliable technology: Gas turbines are known for their robustness and reliability. Even when used, these turbines can continue to operate efficiently for an extended period. They often have a remaining operational life, making them a reliable option for various applications.

  4. Availability of spare parts and expertise: Gas turbines are widely used worldwide, and there is a well-established market for spare parts and technical expertise. Choosing a popular model of a used gas turbine ensures that spare parts and experienced technicians are readily available, simplifying maintenance and repair activities.

  5. 5. Environmental considerations: Utilizing a used gas turbine can contribute to environmental sustainability. By repurposing existing equipment, you are reducing the demand for new manufacturing, which helps conserve resources and reduce carbon emissions associated with production processes.

150 MWh Battery Energy Storage System (BESS)

Green Infrastructure and Power Investments PLC is revolutionizing energy storage with the implementation of a 150 MWh Battery Energy Storage System (BESS). This state-of-the-art system is designed to store and manage large quantities of energy, providing a reliable backup power source and ensuring grid stability.

The BESS is a critical component in the transition to a sustainable energy future. By enabling the storage of excess renewable energy generated during periods of high production, the system ensures that this energy can be used when demand is high or when production is low. This capability is particularly important for renewable energy sources, which are often intermittent by nature. For example, solar power generation peaks during the day but drops off at night, while energy demand can vary significantly throughout the day.

The 150 MWh capacity of the BESS allows it to store substantial amounts of energy, making it a powerful tool for balancing supply and demand on the grid. During periods of low renewable energy generation, the stored energy can be dispatched to meet demand, ensuring a stable and reliable power supply. This functionality enhances grid resilience and helps to prevent outages.

In addition to providing backup power and stabilizing the grid, the BESS also supports the integration of more renewable energy into the grid. By smoothing out the variability of renewable energy sources, the system enables higher penetration of renewables, reducing the need for fossil fuel-based peaking power plants.

This investment in advanced energy storage technology highlights Green Infrastructure and Power Investments PLC’s commitment to innovative solutions in energy storage and management. The BESS not only improves the efficiency and resilience of the energy supply but also facilitates a smoother transition to a sustainable energy future. By investing in cutting-edge storage technologies, the company is positioning itself at the forefront of the renewable energy industry and demonstrating its leadership in the pursuit of sustainable energy solutions.
